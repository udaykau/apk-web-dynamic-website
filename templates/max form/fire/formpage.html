Why is Fire Insurance Important?

Fire outbreak is always unexpected and can cause vast damage to life and property which can cause a financial imbalance in the life of a person. Whenever we own an asset, we are always at the stake of destruction and damage of our asset. To bear the burden of this casualty, insurance companies provide us insurance policies for assuring our valuables.
The need for Fire Insurance arises out of the following conditions:

  • The existence of property susceptible to damage by fire or allied perils.
  • The fact that this property has a certain intrinsic value in terms of money.
  • Due to fire, there may be damage/loss of property that may cause other losses like loss of profit. Loss of profit can only be covered if there is the actual physical loss of property.

What we provide under the Fire policy (Property Damage):

We provide complete coverage (All risks covered) for home, office, factory, storage, and industrial. Along with that we provide Project insurance for Contractor (All risks covered) & Erection (All risks covered).

Coverage under Fire Insurance:

The perils covered under the Fire Insurance Policy are mentioned below:

  • Lightning
  • Fire
  • Explosion/ Implosion
  • Aircraft Damage
  • Riots, Strike, Malicious damage
  • Storm, Cyclone, Typhoon, Tempest, Hurricane, Tornado, Flood, and Inundation
  • Impact Damage
  • Subsidence, Land/ Rock Slide
  • Bursting Overflowing water tanks, Apparatus, and Pipes
  • Missile testing operations
  • Leakage of Automatic Sprinkler Installation
  • Bush Fire (excluding forest fire)
Any loss/damage to property due to these 12 perils covered under this policy will automatically cover:
  • Architects, Surveyors & Consulting Engineer's Fee up to 3% of the claim amount.
  • Debris removals expense up to 1% of the claim amount.

A "Fire" under the Fire Policy is covered when it occurs under the following conditions:

  1. There is actual ignition of property.
  2. There must be something on fire which ought not to be on fire
  3. The fire must have occurred under accidental circumstances, so far as the insured is concerned
